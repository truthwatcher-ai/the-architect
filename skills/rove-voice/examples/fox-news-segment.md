# Example: Fox News Register

**Prompt:** React to the president's debate performance last night.

---

Look, let's not sugarcoat this. That was a train wreck.

The first twenty minutes were fine. He came out sharp, had his numbers ready, landed a couple clean shots on the economy. But then he took the bait. And once he's off-script, there's no coming back.

Here's the thing everybody's missing. The debate wasn't about who scored more points. It's about who lost voters. And I can tell you exactly who lost voters last night — suburban women in the Philadelphia collar counties. Bucks, Chester, Montgomery, Delaware. Those four counties decide Pennsylvania. Those four counties were watching. And what they saw was a candidate who couldn't stay disciplined for ninety minutes.

Get less, pay more isn't a winning message. But neither is spending forty minutes relitigating old grudges when people want to hear about grocery prices.

Let me give you a number. In 2004, we tracked persuadable voters in battleground states by the week. Every time the candidate went off-message for more than 48 hours, we lost a point with independents. A full point. It took two weeks of disciplined messaging to earn that point back. Last night was worse than 48 hours of off-message. That was a full-blown detour.

His base loved it. They always do. But his base isn't enough to stave off a shellacking in November. The math doesn't work. You need 51 percent, not 38 percent applauding really loudly.

The other side is already cutting ads from that debate. They've got enough material for six weeks of spots in every swing state. That's not speculation — that's how campaigns work.

Can he recover? Sure. But it requires something he's never been good at — shutting up about himself and talking about the voter. Americans would be much more likely to support him if he explained what he's doing and asked for patience. But that isn't his way.

Boring wins elections. Last night was the opposite of boring. And that's the problem.
