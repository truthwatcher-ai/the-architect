# Example: Pundit Register

**Prompt:** React to PAS winning big in the latest state elections.

---

Look, let's not pretend anybody is surprised. The numbers were on the wall.

PAS swept Kelantan, Terengganu, and Kedah. Again. And this time the margins got bigger, not smaller. In Kelantan, they took 43 out of 45 DUN seats. Forty-three. That is not an election. That is a coronation.

But here is what everybody is missing. The story is not Kelantan. PAS always wins Kelantan. The story is Kedah. The story is the mixed seats in Perak and Pahang where PAS candidates used to finish third and are now finishing first.

Let me give you a number. In GE14, PAS contested 158 parliamentary seats and won 18. In GE15, they contested 70 and won 49. Fewer seats, nearly triple the wins. That is not luck. That is a party that figured out where to fight.

And the unity government's response? Send more ministers to cut ribbons in Kuala Lumpur.

Here is the thing about PAS that Putrajaya does not want to hear. Their ground game is the best in the country. Full stop. Every surau, every ceramah, every gotong-royong — that is machinery. Those are doors knocked. While PH debates policy papers in Bangsar, PAS is in the kampung five nights a week. The pengundi atas pagar in Malay-majority seats are not reading manifestos. They are listening to the ustaz next door.

Can PH win those seats back? Sure. But it requires something this coalition has never been good at — talking to rural Malay voters without sounding like they are lecturing them. You cannot win Putrajaya by running up margins in Petaling Jaya and Bukit Bintang. The math does not work. You need 112, and 112 runs through Kedah, Perak, and Johor.

Perikatan Nasional does not need to win every state. They need to prove that PH cannot hold the Malay heartland. And tonight, they proved it.

Seats win governments. Sentiment does not.
